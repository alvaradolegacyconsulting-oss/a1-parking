## What this document is (and isn't)

This is a **plain-English operational reference** that maps Texas Chapter 2308 (the Texas Towing and Booting Act) obligations to your day-to-day use of ShieldMyLot™. It's intended to help you understand what the law expects of you and how the platform supports those obligations.

**Compliance with Chapter 2308 is your responsibility, not the platform's.** ShieldMyLot is modeled around Chapter 2308 workflows — the tow ticket fields, evidence capture, dispute paths, and audit trails reflect how the law expects the work to happen. But the platform does NOT certify compliance, does NOT verify your signage, does NOT confirm your authorization agreements are legally sufficient, and does NOT substitute for licensed legal counsel. The operator (your towing company or property management firm) is the regulated party under Chapter 2308; ShieldMyLot is the operational support layer.

**This is not legal advice.** ShieldMyLot is not a law firm. For specific legal questions — especially before making significant decisions about enforcement practices, disputes, or compliance gaps — consult a Texas attorney specializing in property and tow law.

**This document is not a substitute for reading Chapter 2308.** The actual statutory language is available through the Texas Legislature's website and other public sources. Operators should be familiar with the law's actual text.

## What Chapter 2308 governs

Texas Occupations Code Chapter 2308, often called the Texas Towing and Booting Act, regulates non-consent towing and vehicle booting from private property in Texas. It establishes rules for:

- When and how vehicles can be towed from private property
- What signage must be posted at properties where towing is authorized
- Authorization requirements between property owners and tow operators
- Tow ticket and notice content requirements
- Storage facility requirements
- Vehicle owner rights, including the right to dispute a tow
- Penalties for violations

If your business is towing in Texas, every operational decision you make is shaped by Chapter 2308.

ShieldMyLot is a documentation and workflow platform. It does not replace your operational responsibility for:

- **Maintaining proper signage** at each property

- **Operating compliant storage facilities** including licensing, security, and fees

- **Training your drivers** on Chapter 2308 requirements and your company's policies
- **Responding to inquiries from the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation** or other regulators
- **Defending your business** in formal disputes, hearings, or legal proceedings
- **Maintaining appropriate insurance** for the risks of tow operations
- **Staying current on Chapter 2308 amendments** and related Texas case law

Compliance is your business responsibility. The platform supports it but cannot replace it.
